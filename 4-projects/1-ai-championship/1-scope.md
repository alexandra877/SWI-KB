**Start date:** 5 March 2026
**Programme coordinator:** Linnéa Odqvist

## The Goal

> BusTech & Procurement will become GWS's first AI-native division.

## The Why

BusTech & Procurement teams support other Visma employees in their daily operations. Many focus on helping others with AI transformation — but the division needs to adopt AI itself first. You cannot credibly guide others through AI transformation without being AI-native yourself.

## AI Adoption Levels Framework

The programme uses a 4-level AI adoption model (from the central AI Workforce Team):

| Level | Label | What it means | Human role |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Assisted | "Tell me how to do this" — no data manipulation | Human drives, AI assists. User is more efficient. |
| 2 | Automated | "Do this for me in the real world" — real-world data impact | Human plans, AI executes steps. User automates repetitive workflows. |
| 3 | Agentic | "Plan what to do, then execute" — complex cross-functional workflows | Human delegates outcomes, not tasks. User achieves team-level productivity. |
| 4 | AI Native | "Achieve this outcome" — self-improving agent with real-world impact | Human manages strategically. AI handles operational load. |

**Programme goal:** Move every BusTech & Procurement employee meaningfully up this scale during 2026.

## Key Strategic Insights (from March 2026 BT All-Hands)

Lessons from building a production AI agent system internally:

- AI agents need organisational knowledge — without curated memory, they are just generic chatbots
- The hard part is not the AI — it is the data pipeline (ingestion, curation, quality)
- Security is non-trivial: prompt injection is real, PII handling matters, GDPR applies
- Human-in-the-loop is essential — do not fully automate without review gates
- Build to learn, then scale — start with one user, then enable shared access for the team

## Programme Structure

### AI Mentors

17–21 AI Mentors are nominated by team leads — one per team (some larger teams get two). Mentors are the multiplier: they learn first, then teach their team.

Why it works (science):
1. People learn best when they apply immediately
2. The problem is real (not hypothetical)
3. They teach someone else
4. Feedback loops are short

### Support Network

Each AI Mentor is assigned one person from the support network. They meet at least once a month together with the team lead to discuss challenges, feedback, and success stories.

### Fix-it Friday

A recurring 2-hour protected time block where teams pause business-as-usual to fix a broken or repetitive process using AI.

**Format:**
- **Before:** Team members list their "Top 3 Irritants" — repetitive tasks that drain energy. AI Mentor assesses which to tackle.
- **Show (30 min):** AI Mentor explains why they chose the tasks and demonstrates the automation live.
- **Solve (60 min):** Team members try it themselves and estimate time saved per week.
- **Share (30 min):** Show-and-tell — best automations are celebrated and reported to the support network.

**Important:** Always include a dialogue around psychological safety — "When we automate your current tasks, you remain valuable. You will now have more time for more challenging work." This is crucial for people to dare setting up automations.
