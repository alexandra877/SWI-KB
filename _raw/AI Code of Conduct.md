Visma is an AI-forward company. We expect and encourage every employee to use AI tools to work better - to move faster, think sharper, and spend less time on the repetitive tasks, so you can do more of what matters.

Read it, use it, ask questions and provide feedback! The best outcome is a Visma where every company and employee feels empowered by AI, not intimidated by it.

# Care (!) about what you share with the AI model

Connecting to workspace tools
When you connect any tool or integration, including AI tools, third-party apps, plugins, or automation services, to workspace tools ( Drive, Slack, Gmail) or other company systems, you are giving it access to everything in scope: internal communications, colleague personal data, confidential information, and potentially customer production data.

![](GoogleShape2427p238.jpg)

![](GoogleShape2426p238.jpg)

Be aware of customer production data
Confidential and sensitive information
Do not include customer production data unless your customer contract explicitly allows it. Often you also have to notify customers of usage of the AI tool.

If you connect your whole Drive, Gmail or Jira, and it contains customer production data, this will be seen as sharing customer production data with the AI tool.

Do not enter/connect your work tools containing trade secrets, unpublished financials, M&A information, or similar into AI tools unless the vendor contract includes a strong confidentiality clause and prohibits use of data for training.

Please see the Visma reviewed tools list for guidance.

Finding the balance

# Responsible AI
The transformative power of Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a key driver of innovation. At Visma, we believe that a comprehensive approach to AI that takes environmental, social and ethical impacts into consideration, is the very foundation for continued innovation and adoption that can empower us to keep shaping societies in a positive way. AI technologies must always be used in ways that are as efficient as possible, and in line with Visma’s policies and high standards for ethical business conduct, legal compliance, security and sustainability. This includes, but is not limited to, actively meeting the following principles:

Verify output accuracy and accountability: Always cross-check AI output, and never use results you do not understand or cannot explain, especially if the results do not refer to credible sources.
Drive Environmental Efficiency: Practice sustainable and efficient use and development of AI systems, by providing full context in prompts and using the right-sized, most efficient AI tool, in accordance with Visma’s Environmentally Sustainable Use of AI Tools guidelines.
Mitigate Biases: Identify and mitigate biases in AI models and underlying data to ensure all individuals are treated justly and in line with Visma’s Code of Conduct.

# Competition law: Customer data based services
Visma controls large amounts of customer data in the areas of accounting, tax, invoicing, HR and payments. We further have significant market share in several regions and segments
This knowledge gives the potential to build “AI Virtual Experts”, such as a “Virtual CFO” or “Virtual HR manager” services, or AI based Benchmarking Services such as “How does my company perform relative to others”
In abstract this is a category of products and services based on aggregation of non-public customer/client data for purposes other than general product improvement. This differs from similar products based on scraping the web for public information
Such products/services will by default touch upon competition law issues, in particular in markets and segments in the Nordics and The Netherlands where Visma has a near dominant position.

### Notes:

<!-- Slide number: 23 -->
06 Developers & product teams
# Competition law: Customer data based services - 2
Products and services based on aggregation of non-public customer/client data, such as a “Virtual CEO”, are then often offered or shared with the same group of customers who contributed data to the service
Competition authorities may interpret such indirect sharing of financial and behavioural data between customers as a breach of competition law since the customers may use the products and services to reduce competition
Visma customers through a “Virtual CFO” who knows all Visma customer data could get insight into their industry pricing, cost structure and margins that is not publicly available. This could lead them to harmonize prices in a way that may reduce competition
Competition authorities have signaled that this is problematic on principle, regardless of counter arguments of sample sizes or human equivalents

![](GoogleShape2644p252.jpg)

### Notes:

<!-- Slide number: 24 -->
06 Developers & product teams
# Competition law: Customer data based services - 3
Besides the competition authorities, Visma’s customers could also be negative to contribute data opt-in consent to these type of “Virtual CFO” services when they understand that they may contribute to increasing or reducing competition (market share or value share) in their markets
Based on this the Visma Legal & Compliance department recommends that these type of services should begin with, or pivot to
Using publicly available data (e.g. scraping public sources or expert litterature) for training the AI. A smart AI based “Virtual CFO” based on this method would still add value to the customers
When offering AI expert products/services only use the individual customers own data in combination with AI training from public/expert sources to develop AI based services for that individual customer
If using aggregated customer data for training AI experts, then the service should not offer any regional or segment based granularity
