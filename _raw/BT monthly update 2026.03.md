# Demo (Gemini + Google Sites)

![](GoogleShape9815p864.jpg)
🚀 How I Built This (The "Smart Prompt" Method)
Gave Gemini a "Job": I didn't just ask for a calculator. I told Gemini to act like a Financial Expert and a Web Developer at the same time.
Defined the "Big Three": I made sure the AI focused on the three things that matter most in a contract: Cost (what we pay), Usage (how many people use it), and Value (what we get back).
Requested "Plug-and-Play" Code: I told the AI to write the code in a special "all-in-one" format so I could just copy and paste it into our Google Site without needing to be a programmer.
Added "Executive Logic": I asked the AI to include the actual math formulas on the screen so everyone can see exactly how the ROI (Return on Investment) is calculated.
Focused on the "Break-Even": I instructed the AI to highlight the exact moment the tool starts making us money,

Fix-it Friday

An Example of a Train the Trainer Session
“Fix-it Friday gave me the time to stop doing that one manual task I've known I could automate for months, but not got time to prioritize!”
Concept:
A 2-hour protected time block where teams pause "business as usual" to fix a broken or repetitive process using AI.

![](GoogleShape10070p876.jpg)
Format:
Before the Session: Team members list their "Top 3 Irritants"—repetitive tasks that drain energy (e.g., "Manually copying data from Jira to Sheets"). AI Mentor assess what tasks to deal with.
Show (30 min): AI Mentor explain why she choose the tasks she did to automate. After that the AI Mentor shows how a team member can automate this task with the help of an Agentic tool.

Solve (60 min): The team members get to try it themselves and estimate how much time this will save them in the upcoming week.
For activities like this we need to make sure to include a dialogue of “When we now automate your current tasks you as an employee will still be valuable for the company. You will now get more time to spend on more challenging tasks - So we get even better use of your expertise”. This is crucial to create psychological safety and make people dare setting up automations.
Share (30 min): A "Show and Tell" session where solutions are demonstrated. The best automations are celebrated and presented to the support people later on.
